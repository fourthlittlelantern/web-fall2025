About Asghbgahn

A collection of information, written and collected by The Tour Guide.

Welcome to Asghbgahn!

Welcome to Asghbgahn, dear traveler! We sincerely hope you enjoy your time here in our lovely kingdom. In this booklet, you will find all sorts of helpful information regarding our kingdom and how things work around here.

PLEASE READ CAREFULLY.


The Kingdom of Asghbgahn

Asghbgahn is located on the northern edges of the continent, isolated from other kingdoms by a long stretch of Interlands.


A Brief History

Asghbgahn has always existed. It has always been the same, for as long as anyone can remember. That is why we have no history books. There is simultaneously too much and too little to write about.

A widely believed legend, though, is that Asghbgahn was built on the ruins of an ancient empire. What this great empire was, or why it collapsed, remains a mystery.


Rules

Most of these are not laws, but basic courtesies, common observances, and other things that are worth noting if you want to understand our kingdom. You will likely come across most, if not all, of these on your journey.

  1. The rule of Asghbgahn. Everything is Asghbgahn. Asghbgahn is everything.
  2. All directions point to north. All is north. North is all.
  3. Everything has significance. Everything is Asghbgahn.
  4. The rules shall not be questioned.
  5. Asghbgahn.

The King

Yes, Asghbgahn has a king. He resides in The Capital, or at least he used to. No one really knows anymore.

The point is, this title is mostly nominal. The king does mostly nothing. The kingdom runs itself, for there is not much to even run in the first place.


Cycles

You may have heard references to something called cycles (last cycle, 4 cycles ago, etc.). Cycles are simply the customary way of measuring time beyond what is considered present day. Think of it as eras.

We do not count years in past cycles. Why bother? There is not much to count. Nothing distinguishes one year from another. For most, the years all blend together.

How long is a cycle? It varies. It seems to vary, at least. That is a common, reasonable assumption. Once a cycle has ended, there is no way to look back and find out. The count has reset. The years of that cycle no longer exist.
